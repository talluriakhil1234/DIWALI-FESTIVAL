Diwali Celebrations in India

Discover the various ways Diwali, the Festival of Lights, is celebrated across India!

Overview of Diwali

Event Description Image
Festival Date Diwali usually falls in October or November, marking the new moon of the Hindu month Kartika.
Significance Diwali celebrates the return of Lord Rama to Ayodhya and the triumph of light over darkness.

Diwali Rituals and Traditions

Ritual Description Image
Lighting Diyas Earthen lamps, or diyas, are lit in homes to signify the victory of light over darkness.
Rangoli Making Colorful Rangolis are created at home entrances to welcome prosperity and good luck.
Fireworks People celebrate with fireworks, lighting up the night sky to ward off evil spirits.

Diwali Sweets and Treats

Sweet Description Image
Ladoo A traditional sweet made of gram flour, sugar, and ghee, enjoyed during Diwali.
Barfi Sweet, dense treats made with milk solids and sugar, often decorated with silver leaf.
Jalebi A crispy, syrupy sweet that is popular across India, especially during festive seasons.
